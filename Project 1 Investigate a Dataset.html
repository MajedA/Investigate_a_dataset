Introduction¶

In this project, The Movie Database dataset was chosen in attempt to explore different attributes of the data to find different types of relations an try to analyze those relations.
The set is consists of over 10,000 data records of movies from TMDB archive.



TMDB.ORG is a crowd-sourced movie information database used by many film-related consoles, sites and apps, such as XBMC, MythTV and Plex. Dozens of media managers, mobile apps and social sites make use of its API.
TMDb lists over 80,000 films at time of writing, which is considerably fewer than IMDb. While not as complete as IMDb, it holds extensive information for most popular/ Hollywood films.

Research Questions:¶

  1. Does the audience prefare to sit through a long feature (over 90 minutes) or they prefare shorter runtimes?

  2. Among all the movie genres in the sample data, which movie genre is the most popular?

    Issues with previous data¶

    Some of the categories I wish would have been combined to have more clear classifications. For example there is Thriller, Action, Crime, and Horror catigories as genres which all somewhat can be worked to one or two genres.
    Also, the "TV Movie" does not sound much as a genres and more as another type of medium of which viewers can view movies. So I believe it should've not be included under "genres" and be just a movie, regardless if it had a theatrical release, TV movie, or straight-to-DVD/BR type of movies. In my opinion, this affects the accuracy of the results.
    Another issue was that in order to investigate best genre, at first I though of using the popularity data, however, due to the nature of float type it made it difficult to obtain the desired results and eventually decided to count the number of movies made for each genre, which I believe it showed pretty complete result.

First, let's say that I barely scartch the serface of what it can be gathered from the provided dataset.
